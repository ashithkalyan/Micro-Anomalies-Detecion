Event-Driven Architecture

Research Abstract: Micro-Anomaly Detection

This section introduces the core concept of event-driven anomaly detection in agricultural water pumps. Unlike traditional systems that continuously stream heavy data, our approach establishes a baseline signature and only activates upon detecting micro-behavioral changes. This research focuses on the algorithm development, feature extraction, and end-user translation required to make this sensor a reality.

🎧

The Problem

Existing systems detect faults too late (macro-level failure) and are expensive due to continuous data transmission. Farmers suffer crop loss due to sudden pump breakdowns.

🧠

Our Methodology

Implement embedded DSP (Digital Signal Processing). We apply bandpass filtering and envelope analysis to acoustic/vibration signals to isolate micro-frequency patterns representing early wear.

📱

The Deliverable

A lightweight fault classification model that operates completely offline, converting complex vibration matrices into simple, actionable SMS/App alerts for farmers.

Research Scope (Current Phase): Selecting specific feature extraction libraries, defining the mathematical limits for anomaly thresholds, and mapping specific pump mechanical dynamics (Imbalance, Cavitation, Bearing Wear) to UI outputs.

Statistical Feature Extraction & Classification

To run on low-power edge devices, we cannot use heavy Deep Learning. Instead, we extract statistical features (RMS, Kurtosis, Crest Factor) from the filtered signal. Our classification model uses these parameters to identify the specific mechanical failure mode.

Feature Footprint Matrix

Comparison of statistical feature intensities across different fault types.

Healthy Baseline ✅

Low RMS (overall energy), normal crest factor. The machine is running smoothly within baseline tolerances.

Shaft Imbalance ⚠️

High Peak value at 1x RPM running speed. Causes a heavy sinusoidal wave. High RMS, lower Kurtosis.

Pump Cavitation 🫧

Random high-frequency broad-band noise caused by collapsing bubbles. Moderate RMS, High Variance.

Bearing Defect ⚙️

Characterized by sharp, repetitive impacts. Very High Kurtosis (spikiness) and High Crest Factor.

Farmer-Friendly Actionable Alerts

The final output of our event-driven system. The sensor remains silent unless an anomaly passes the classification threshold. The complex mathematics are translated into simple, color-coded, actionable advice for the farmer.

📡

1. Edge Device Trigger

Sensor detects abnormal signal pattern (e.g., High Kurtosis). Wakes up from sleep mode.

🧮

2. On-board Classification

Algorithm classifies the fault as "Outer Race Bearing Wear" with 85% confidence.

💬

3. Translation to User

System maps the fault to a pre-defined mitigation strategy and transmits a low-bandwidth SMS/App alert.
